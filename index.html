Road Collisions in Queensland

Looking into the trends regarding road collisions in Queensland between 2001 and 2018

As an overview, the number of casualities over the years are relatively low, with most collisions occuring around Brisbane. The high density of collisions around Brisbane and the Gold Coast compliments the fact these are major cities, major tourist locations and have heavy traffic conditions throughout the years. Collisions tend to occur along the coastline and this coincides with high traffic areas as the major towns in Queensland are located along the coast.

In terms of the severity of collisions, the road accidents tend to occur mostly in a 60km/h speed limt with the least number of collision occuring in a 70km/h speed limit. Proportionally, the most about of fatalities come from higher speed collisions.

Considering the time of day, the number of collisions are higher around 8am and between 3-5pm. This trend is as expected as these are peak traffic hours in which more vehicles are on the roads, regardless of which month of the year.
